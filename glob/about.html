roshambo is a gall app with a web interface and a dojo interface

gall app

most of the logic is to accomplish the following:

if two users tell their agents to %poise against one another, the agents will chat until they settle on a time window.

they'll set %behn timers for t0, and theyll listen for the opponent from t0 until t0+n

-

then theres some ugly stuff about how I'm dealing with http-api inputs & conditional (?verbose) debug prints.

web interface

This frontend is hand-written HTML/CSS/JS bundled with http-api and urbit-ob using browserify.

The UI logic is a short (messy) list of click event functions and a few calls to http-api.

dojo interface

Step 1: set the app to verbose mode.

:roshambo|verbose &

Step 2: shoot

:roshambo|shoot %r
:roshambo|shoot %p
:roshambo|shoot %s

Step 3: poise

:roshambo|poise ~sampel-palnet

When you and an opponent poise against one another, your agents negotiate an exact time at which they will share shoot values.

this time will be printed to the screen. You can change your shoot any time before the shoot-time.


Step 4: give it a sec

If successful, the results will be printed to the screen sometime between shoot-time and shoot-time + latency.

Otherwise you may need to tweak your latency and poise-delay values.

Other commands

latency and poise-delay are @dr values used in negotiating a game. They have default values which may not be ideal for play depending on the quality of your p2p connection. These could be increased for a slow connection, or decreased for a fast connection.

:roshambo|latency ~sX

sets your latency value to X seconds.

If I have a latency of X seconds, my agent will accept a shoot until X seconds after the shoot-time.

This can be generous. Too short (< actual_latency) and your shoots won't make it in time.

If its too long (> 2 * actual_latency), then someone with a modded %roshambo could technically cheat. But thats pretty unlikely.


:roshambo|poise-delay ~sY

sets your poise-delay value to Y seconds.

If I have a poise-delay of Y seconds, the shoot-time for my game will be at least Y seconds after I poise.

Give it enough for your agents to poke back and forth a few times.

Agents settle on the larger poise-delay.
